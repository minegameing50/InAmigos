About InAmigos Foundation

Founded September 23, 2020 | Registered under Section 8

InAmigos Foundation was founded on September 23, 2020, by Mr. Govind Shukla (Founder & CEO). It is a Section 8 registered non-profit organization, licensed by the Central Government, based in Bilaspur, Chhattisgarh.

It holds 80G & 12A certifications, ensuring transparency, accountability and tax-exempt benefits for donors. Our foundation is also CSR-1 registered, allowing collaboration with corporate partners. Additionally, we are NITI Aayog registered and hold the prestigious ISO 9001:2015 certification, signifying our commitment to high-quality standards in operations.

At InAmigos Foundation, we believe in the power of collective action. Every contribution is directed towards food distribution, education, women empowerment, animal welfare, environmental sustainability and social inclusion programs across India.

Project Prakriti

Environmental Initiative
Impact: 20,000+ Trees Planted

Project Prakriti represents our commitment to environmental sustainability. Operating primary drives across Chhattisgarh, our volunteers organize street-level tree plantation campaigns, promote public plastic-free zones by distributing cloth/jute bags, and install dustbins and waste segregation points in municipal areas. We believe in building a clean, green future by educating young children and communities on waste management and active ecosystem recovery.

Project Seva

Humanitarian Relief
Impact: 50,000+ Meals Distributed

Project Seva combats food insecurity directly at the grassroots level. Our work includes daily hot meal distributions in slum regions and orphanage facilities, dry ration kit supplies to low-income families, and nutritional kits tailored specifically for pregnant women and infants. We also manage active "no food waste" drives to collect and re-route surplus food from events directly to communities in need.

Project Udaan

Women Empowerment
Impact: 900+ Girls Empowered

Project Udaan targets empowerment through education, vocational opportunities, and digital campaigns. We run programs supporting women's rights awareness, suicide prevention, and mental health resources. Our digital literacy courses equip school-age girls with key technological skillsets, and we provide financial aid, school supplies, and vocational training sponsorships to underprivileged young women.

Project Jeev

Animal Welfare
Impact: Feeding 50+ Animals Daily

Project Jeev operates with the philosophy that every life matters. We organize daily feeding runs for street animals, provide critical veterinary assistance and vaccines for injured stray dogs/cats, and coordinate community education seminars to encourage stray adoption and local animal care. During lockdowns and crises, Project Jeev expanded operations to ensure that animal populations received regular food supplies.

Bachpan Shala

Rural Education
Target: Primary Education Access

Bachpan Shala bridges the critical literacy gap in rural and slum communities. We sponsor education for underprivileged children by providing complete schooling sets (textbooks, uniforms, writing pads, and fees), conducting free mobile outdoor study blocks in remote parts of Bilaspur, and operating community teaching drives. Our volunteers provide vital foundational support to ensure no child is left behind.
